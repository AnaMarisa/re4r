ABOUT

Survival is
just the beginning

Operation Baby Eagle originally consisted of one sole seasoned agent from a top-secret organization, Leon S. Kennedy, who were sent on a mission to find the kidnapped daughter of the President, Ashley Graham (codename: Baby Eagle) who had gone missing and was last seen in Valdelobos.

Upon arriving in Valdelobos, Leon soon learned that there was more at stake than just the President's daughter. The Valdelobos village was home to a breed of parasitic super organisms called the Las Plagas.

At this point, Leon met Ada Wong, who used to assist him in his previous mission, but happened to be tasked to retrieve a sample of Las Plagas.

The further Leon goes into Valdelobos, contact between him and the Division of Security Operations gradually disappeared.

Fortunately, the United States was at least informed about Las Plagas. Worried of losing their well-respected agent as well as the safety of Baby Eagle, the Division of Security Operations started recruiting more agents from different sectors, primarily with the aim to reconnect back with Leon and complete the mission.

Eventually, the team discovered that Ashley had been kidnapped by the Los Illuminados cult that operated in the area.

In Valdelobos, they were assisted by Luis, a local resident who knew the area like the back of his hand. Luis was invaluable to the team, helping them navigate through the village’s labyrinthine streets and providing vital information about the cult’s movements.

With the mass deployment of such number of agents and the danger of Los Illuminados, will everybody will be able to finish the operation and escape safely from Valdelobos? Or, will they be consumed and sacrificed to the darkness along with Baby Eagle?

REGULATIONS

Pledges to be honored

  1. 1

    Maintain absolute secrecy: As a secret agent, you must keep your identity, mission details, and other sensitive information a secret. You should never discuss your work with anyone who is not authorized to know about it, and should take steps to ensure that you cannot be easily identified or traced.

  2. 2

    Follow the law: While secret agents may sometimes operate in grey areas, they are still bound by the law of the country in which they are operating. You must comply with all applicable laws and regulations, and avoid engaging in any activities that would be considered illegal or unethical.

  3. 3

    Prioritize national security: Your mission as a secret agent is to protect national security and advance the interests of your country. This should always be your top priority, and you should be prepared to make sacrifices and take risks to achieve your objectives.

  4. 4

    Use discretion: When conducting surveillance or other operations, you must use discretion and avoid drawing attention to yourself. You should also be careful not to disrupt or interfere with the lives of innocent civilians who may be caught up in your activities.

  5. 5

    Maintain professionalism: As a secret agent, you represent your country and your agency. You must conduct yourself in a professional and ethical manner at all times, and avoid engaging in any behavior that could damage your reputation or that of your organization.
